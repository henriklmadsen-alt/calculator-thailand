## Quality Standards for Thai Content

### Tone
- ✅ Warm, approachable, expert authority
- ✅ User-focused (benefits, solutions, help)
- ❌ Avoid: Corporate jargon, marketing speak, overly formal

### Language
- ✅ Natural Thai (not "translated from English")
- ✅ Real-life examples with Thai context (salaries, costs, dates)
- ✅ Clear, direct sentences
- ❌ Avoid: Machine translation markers, English words without Thai equivalents, inconsistent formal/informal

### Sources & Accuracy
- ✅ Cite government sources where relevant (RD, BOT, NSO, DLT, REB)
- ✅ Use real Thai salary ranges, tax rates, regulations
- ✅ Link to calculator pages as verification tools
- ❌ Avoid: Made-up figures, outdated regulations, generic "Thailand" advice

### Internal Linking
- ✅ Link naturally to 3-7 related calculators per 1000-word article
- ✅ Use descriptive anchor text (link text describes the calculator)
- ✅ Context: Explain why that calculator solves the problem
- ❌ Avoid: "Click here", generic "calculator", too many links in one section

## Common Refresh Triggers

### Scenario 1: Keyword Ranking Drops
**Trigger:** Position drops >3 spots  
**Likely cause:** Content no longer matches search intent, or competitor content better answers the query  
**Your action:** Review query intent, strengthen explanation sections, add better examples, ensure H1/intro clearly answer the question  

### Scenario 2: Low CTR Despite Good Ranking
**Trigger:** Position top 5-10 but CTR <3%  
**Likely cause:** Meta description doesn't match intent, or title doesn't promise clear benefit  
**Your action:** CMO + SEO Specialist update metadata; you update article intro/content to match  

### Scenario 3: New Opportunity Keyword
**Trigger:** High-intent keyword (vol >100/mo) not yet ranking well  
**Likely cause:** New query or unmet search intent  
**Your action:** Write new article or significantly expand existing article to cover this intent  

### Scenario 4: New Algorithm Update Decline
**Trigger:** Tier 1 keyword drops after Google update  
**Likely cause:** Content quality issue (thin explanation, outdated info, poor E-E-A-T signals)  
**Your action:** Enhance article: better sources, expert attribution, real examples, calculator integration

## FAQ

**Q: How long should articles be?**  
A: 800-1,200 words standard. Longer (1,500+) if 3-5+ calculator integrations, shorter (600-800) if focused FAQ-style.

**Q: What if I disagree with a keyword brief?**  
A: Comment on CAL-1624 with your concern. Don't write an article you think is misaligned.

**Q: How do I handle calculator links?**  
A: Natural context. Example: "ใช้[เครื่องคำนวณสินเชื่อ] เพื่อเปรียบเทียบ" (Use [Loan calculator] to compare). Avoid: "Click here" or generic.

**Q: What if an article doesn't improve ranking?**  
A: Common. Try: Better title/meta intent match, more detailed examples, better FAQ, or brand new angle article. Review with CMO/SEO.

**Q: How many articles per week?**  
A: Start: 1-2 per week (refresh + new). Scale up as process improves. Quality > quantity.
