## STYLE GUIDE FOR TRANSLATORS

### Numbers & Currency
- **Currency**: Always write "THB" (Thai Baht), not "Baht" or "฿" alone
  - Example: "1,500,000 THB" not "1,500,000 Baht" or "฿1.5M"
- **Decimal formatting**: Use period (.) for decimals, comma (,) for thousands
  - Example: "1,234,567.50 THB"
- **Percentages**: "5.5% per year" or "5.5 percent annually"

### Tone & Voice
- **Clear over formal**: "Calculate your tax" not "Perform taxable income computation"
- **Helper tone**: "You might also deduct..." not "The taxpayer may optionally claim..."
- **Active voice**: "Enter your salary" not "Salary should be entered"
- **Avoid legal jargon**: Explain first, abbreviate second
  - Good: "Medical Expense Relief (MDR) — healthcare costs up to 15,000 THB"
  - Bad: "MDR per Section 41(2) of the Revenue Code"

### Technical & Financial Accuracy
- **Match official Thai terminology** where possible (e.g., "Social Security" for "สลส.")
- **Use consistent abbreviations**: VAT, MDR, APR (define once, reuse)
- **Avoid false friends**: Thai "ราคา" = Price/Cost, not "Rate" (ราคา ≠ อัตรา)

### Disclaimers
All calculator pages include a disclaimer. Translate consistently:
- English standard: "This calculator is for educational purposes only and is not official tax or financial advice."
- Ensure this message is in every calculator translation.

## GAPS TO CLARIFY (With Translators)

**Before translation starts, confirm with translators on these unclear terms:**

1. **หัก สลส.** — Are we calling this "Social Security Deduction" or "Social Security Contribution"? (Both are used; confirm preference)
2. **ค่าใช้สอย** — In tax context, is this "standard deduction" (ค่าใช้สอย 60K) or "personal exemption"? (Clarify in glossary)
3. **ประกอบการ** — For freelance/self-employment, do we use "Self-Employment" or "Supplementary Income"?
4. **มรดก** vs **สืบทอด** — Are these both "Inheritance" or does one mean "Estate"?
5. **ชั้นการใช้** (tiered rates) — For electricity, should we say "Usage Tier," "Rate Bracket," or "Pricing Tier"?
