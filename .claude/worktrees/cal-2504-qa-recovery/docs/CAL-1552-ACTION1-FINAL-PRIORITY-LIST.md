## Writing Guidelines (For Thai Content Specialist Alpha)

### Quality Standards:
- ✅ **Natural Thai**: Conversational, not mechanical. Real language people use.
- ✅ **Useful Context**: Article explains WHY, not just HOW CALCULATOR WORKS
- ✅ **Examples**: Real Thai scenarios (salary example, business case, etc.)
- ✅ **Length**: 800-1500 words per article (as noted in Duration column)
- ✅ **Links**: Each article links to 2-3 related calculators (internal linking)
- ✅ **No Filler**: No "Top 10 ways to save money" padding. Value-driven only.

### Structure (Typical):
1. **Intro** (100-150w): Why this calculation matters in Thai context
2. **Explanation** (300-400w): Clear explanation + step-by-step examples
3. **Calculator Use** (200-300w): How to use the calculator, common scenarios
4. **Tips/Best Practices** (200-300w): Thailand-specific advice, gotchas, best practices
5. **Related Resources** (100-150w): Link to related calculator articles + calculator tool

### Avoid:
- 🚫 Generic "how to manage money" content
- 🚫 Duplicate of calculator page explanation
- 🚫 Outdated Thai tax/salary rules (verify current 2026 rates)
- 🚫 Articles that don't link to calculator or related content
